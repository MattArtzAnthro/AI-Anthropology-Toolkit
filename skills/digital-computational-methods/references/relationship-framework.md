# The Three Relationships: A Framework for Machine-Involved Research

Reference file for the digital-computational-methods skill. Loaded when
diagnosing which relationship a project enacts with digital technology
and AI, and when a researcher needs the disciplinary history that
legitimates each register.

---

## The Diagnostic

Anthropological projects involve machines in three distinguishable ways
(Artz 2026a):

1. **Studying it.** Digital technology and the social relations it
   generates are the object of inquiry and often the field site itself:
   platform cultures, algorithmic systems, online sociality, digitally
   mediated life (Horst and Miller 2012; Geismar and Knox 2021). The
   stance is interpretive and frequently critical — attentive to how
   digital infrastructures encode assumptions and redistribute power
   (Seaver 2022).
2. **Computing with it.** Computation is an instrument of the
   researcher's own analysis, executing specified operations over
   cultural material at a scale and speed manual methods cannot reach —
   topic modeling, network analysis, machine learning for cultural
   explication (Munk, Olesen, and Jacomy 2022; Munk and Winthereik 2022;
   Breslin and Albris 2026). The machine's part is fixed once analysis
   begins: it executes, the researcher reads the results.
3. **Collaborating with it.** An agentic AI system participates in
   interpretation: it converses, can be steered in real time, makes
   analytical decisions within its scope, and its readings — especially
   its divergent or discomfiting ones — become part of the interpretive
   process. This is the shift from tool to interlocutor that defines AI
   anthropology as "the integrated practice of studying, using, and
   shaping AI" (Artz 2026a).

The test is the relationship the project *enacts*, not the technology it
uses. The same large language model can be object (a community's
sense-making about AI), instrument (classifying a corpus on
instruction), or interlocutor (reasoning toward an interpretation with
the researcher).

## Why the History Matters

Researchers proposing machine-involved work — especially
AI-collaborative work — often meet the objection that it is not
anthropology. The history says otherwise (Artz 2026a; VandenBroek, Artz,
and Koycheva 2026):

- **Cybernetics era.** Anthropologists were present at the founding:
  Margaret Mead and Gregory Bateson took part in the Macy Conferences on
  cybernetics alongside von Neumann and Shannon (Wiener 1948), asking
  how an observer might be part of the system observed.
- **Early computation.** Hymes (1965) advocated computers in linguistic
  anthropology; Kippen (1988) built an AI system to analyze North Indian
  tabla drumming; Cunningham (1997) applied machine learning to kinship
  structures — computation extending analysis without replacing
  interpretive judgment.
- **Embedded engagement.** Suchman (1987) and Forsythe (1993, 1996)
  worked inside AI laboratories, simultaneously studying the systems,
  shaping their development through findings, and demonstrating that
  human action and expert knowledge resist the formalizations AI
  designers assumed — studying, using, and shaping at once.
- **Critical distance.** From the early 2000s through the mid-2010s the
  dominant posture narrowed toward examining AI's sociocultural
  consequences from outside (Seaver 2017, 2022) — valuable, but the
  methodological and applied dimensions receded.
- **Reintegration.** Since roughly 2018, techno-anthropological work —
  notably at Aalborg's TANTLab — has treated computation and thick
  description as complementary impulses: computational ethnography,
  controversy mapping, machine learning for cultural explication
  (Jensen 2013; Munk and Winthereik 2022; Munk, Olesen, and Jacomy
  2022).

The collaborative register is therefore a *recovered* disciplinary
posture — the integrated engagement of the cybernetics and embedded
eras, renewed for systems that can now talk back — not a rupture with
tradition.

## The Registers Inform Each Other

The three relationships operate as a loop rather than a menu (Artz
2026a, 2026b): critical study of AI reveals assumptions that should
guide how systems are used and built; the experience of using and
building generates insights that purely external analysis cannot access.
Practically, this means:

- Re-diagnose the register at phase boundaries. A project that begins by
  studying a platform may compute over its trace data, then bring AI
  into interpreting the results — each shift changes the obligations.
- Findings travel between registers: what the critical reading shows
  about a platform's politics belongs in the computational design; what
  collaboration with a system reveals about its tendencies belongs in
  the critique.

## Disconcertment: The Collaborative Register's Key Principle

When AI participates in interpretation, its value lies substantially in
productive divergence — what Verran and Christie (2013) call
"disconcertment." Documented forms:

- **Synthetic interlocutors.** Søltoft, Kocksch, and Munk (2024)
  connected an LLM to ethnographic archives via retrieval-augmented
  generation, letting researchers converse with their data. The systems
  proved valuable not by reproducing fieldwork but by *reopening* it:
  clean authoritative answers threw into relief the hesitation that had
  characterized the actual interviews; unexpected connections surfaced
  across a polyvocal archive no single researcher had read entire.
- **Friction by design.** Multi-agent ethnography configures AI agents
  under competing analytical lenses and requires the researcher to
  adjudicate their divergent readings (Artz 2026c) — the disagreement is
  sustained as data, not resolved as noise. Slowing reasoning down with
  machine friction is itself a documented technique (Madsen, Munk, and
  Søltoft 2023).

Design implication: build divergence in deliberately (multiple lenses,
multiple readings), record the moments a machine reading feels wrong,
and treat those moments as clarifying what the researcher actually
thinks — that is where the analysis advances.
